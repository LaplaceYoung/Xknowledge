## Patterns Extracted
1. Hero copy is outcome-first, not feature-first.
2. CTA hierarchy is strict: one primary action, one secondary social-proof action.
3. Mid-page proof modules (stats, comparison, quotes) reduce decision friction.
4. Fast activation checklist outperforms long paragraphs for first-use confidence.
5. FAQ appears before final CTA to absorb objection handling.
6. Mobile sticky CTA keeps conversion path visible without scroll-back.
7. Referral/share mechanics often appear as lightweight utility blocks.
